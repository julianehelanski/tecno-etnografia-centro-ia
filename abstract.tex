  \large{\textbf{Abstract}}

What do scientists and engineers do when they develop artificial intelligence? Driven by this question, I followed researchers over four years of ethnographic fieldwork at the Center for Artificial Intelligence of the University of São Paulo, one of the Applied Research Centers of the São Paulo Research Foundation. The center was created in 2020 through an agreement between the foundation, the university, and International Business Machines Corporation. When I began the research in 2022, the partnership seemed stable; when I concluded it in 2025, the corporation had terminated the agreement. The extended field period allowed me to describe the arrangement of technoscientific practices in the sociotechnical network that associated public university and transnational corporation in artificial intelligence research in Brazil, from foundation to dissolution. By following Fábio and Cláudio, directors of the Center, the research traversed the history of the relationship between the center and the corporation and the corporate technoscientific practices over time. By following Marcelo Finger, coordinator of the Early Detection System for Respiratory Insufficiency based on Audio Analysis project, the research traced the translation chain that converted speech recordings of people hospitalized with Covid-19 into spectrograms (visual representations of sound) processed by neural networks for detecting respiratory insufficiency through voice analysis. This ethnographic movement of following actors wherever they led took the thesis through various sites, and it was this movement that led to the choice of actor-network theory as a theoretical-methodological orientation capable of accounting for the field. Throughout the research, the production of ethnographic knowledge mobilized a set of technoscientific inscription practices: interactive diagrams in Mermaid, analyses in Python, textual composition with language models (Claude, Anthropic), spectrograms, website construction for thesis defense presentation. These technoscientific graphies constitute what the thesis names techno-ethnography, designating the research mode that inhabits of tension between the technical circulability of inscriptions and the sensible reality of human bodies. The research contributes to science and technology studies by demonstrating that following multiple actors in an artificial intelligence research center makes it possible to analyze both the institutional and corporate negotiations that sustain artificial intelligence research and the situated technoscientific practices of systems development, investigating how these two levels articulate in the production of knowledge in artificial intelligence and in the production of knowledge about artificial intelligence by the social sciences.
\noindent \textbf{Keywords}: Techno-ethnography; Technoscience; Artificial Intelligence; Technoscientific practice; Sociotechnical network.
